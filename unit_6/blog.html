Meet Rick Campanella.

“I started thinking about photography fairly early in life. While growing up it seemed that every time my family posed for a photo, I was asked to do the honors. When I was 10 I got a little camera but soon realized that I couldn’t pay for film developing so I never got to use it. I was almost 30 by the time I bought a 35mm Nikon and learned film and slide photography, and got a bit of recognition by my camera club. Within a decade, the Digital Revolution had begun and it turned me off photography. I hated the clunky digital cameras and that their lenses weren’t interchangeable. And of course digital image manipulation software seemed to cheapen the “purity” of film photography". Read more
